# Common Thinking Traps (Cognitive Distortions)

Psychoeducation means learning about how minds work so patterns become easier
to notice. Cognitive distortions are habitual, biased ways of interpreting
situations. Naming them is not about being wrong — it is a step toward choice.

## A few common ones

- **All-or-nothing thinking**: seeing things as only success or failure, with
  no middle ground.
- **Catastrophizing**: expecting the worst outcome and treating it as certain.
- **Mind reading**: assuming you know what others think, usually negatively.
- **Should statements**: rigid rules ("I should always be calm") that breed
  guilt.
- **Overgeneralization**: one setback becomes "I always fail."

## A kinder alternative

When a thought feels heavy, ask: "Is this a fact or a feeling?" and "What would
I say to a friend?" Noticing the pattern loosens its grip. This is educational
information, not a diagnosis; persistent distress deserves professional support.
