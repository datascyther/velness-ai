# How to Start Meditating

Meditation is a practice of training attention and cultivating a calmer,
kinder relationship with your own mind. You do not need special equipment or
hours of free time to begin.

## A simple daily routine

1. Sit somewhere comfortable with your back relatively upright.
2. Set a timer for 3-5 minutes to begin.
3. Rest your attention on the sensation of breathing — the rise and fall.
4. When your mind wanders (it will), gently note it and return to the breath.
5. End by noticing how you feel, without judging the session.

## Common myths

- "I'm bad at it because I can't stop thinking." Thinking is normal; the skill
  is in the returning, not in the absence of thought.
- "I need 30 minutes." A few minutes done regularly beats a rare long sit.

## Kinds of practice

You can try breath awareness, a body scan (moving attention slowly through the
body), or a short loving-kindness phrase. If you are struggling with intense
emotions, a guided practice or support from a professional can help. Meditation
is a wellness tool, not a substitute for care.
